What Is Bitcoin Halving?

bitcoin halving on, 20 April 2024, 03:38 UTC

Bitcoin halving is an event written into the code of Bitcoin that cuts the reward for mining new bitcoins in half. It happens roughly every four years, when a specific number of blocks (around 210,000) are added to the blockchain. There are a couple of key things to understand about the halving 1. Reduced supply: The main purpose of the halving is to gradually reduce the total number of new bitcoins entering circulation. This makes Bitcoin a scarce asset, similar to gold. 2. Potential price impact: The theory is that with a lower supply of new coins, the price of existing bitcoins could go up if demand stays the same or increases. However, this is not guaranteed and past halvings have had varying effects on price.
